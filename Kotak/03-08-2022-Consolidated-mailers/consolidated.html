Let’s go over 5 of the most important things to keep in mind when investing, or saving!
 Emergency fund
Emergency fund is an amount of money saved, to be used in the case of any emergency, or unforeseen circumstances. As a rule of thumb, it’s a good idea to have a fund which can carry you through 6 months.
Best places to start an emergency fund:
 • Savings Account
 • Fixed Deposit
 • Liquid Fund
 Investing
Investing is a reliable way to secure your future, and has the potential to introduce you to big returns as well! We’d recommend these 2 avenues for first-time investors:

  • Equity Funds
  • Debt Funds

Furthermore, there are alternatives to these, such as cryptocurrency, gold, and foreign stocks.
 Tax Saving
There are various tax-saving avenues available to everyone out there, all it takes is to understand the conditions. These are the sections in the Income Tax Act of 1961 to delve into further, should you be curious about stocks:
 • Section 80C
 • Section 80CCD
 • Section 80CCD (1B)
 • Section 80D
 • Section 80E
 • Section 80EEA
 Credit Score
Your Credit Score is a crucial factor in determining eligibility for various crucial facets of finance, including credit cards, and loan approvals! Here are some tactics you can employ to boost your score:

  • Pay EMI’s on time
  • Limit total debt
  • Opt for longer repayment periods
  • Opt for a mix of debt types
  • Check your credit report
 Benefits of NPS
Lastly, you can secure your post-retirement through the National Pension System. The benefits of which are:
 1. Additional tax benefit of ₹15,600* u/s 80 CCD(1B) on investing ₹50,000 annually.
 2. Start an SIP with as low as ₹500 per month.
 3. A foolproof Government regulated retirement plan
 4. Transparent & Low cost
