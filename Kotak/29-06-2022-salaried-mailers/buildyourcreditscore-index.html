Credit scores can appear complex, and are critical to so many basic banking functions, such as taking out loans, and possessing higher-tier credit cards!

On the flipside, having poor credit scores can damage your credibility in the eyes of financial institutions, leading to low approval rates for loans, and higher interest rates as well!

As can be seen, credit scores can make or break your future plans, and having an eye on it is critical, especially from a young age.
Here are 5 ways to improve your credit score!
 Paying EMI’s on time
The #1 way for young-people to boost their credit score, is by staying
on top of their EMI payments! Should you be late on these payments, which admittedly are easy to miss at times, it can negatively impact
your score!
 Limit your total debt
While many people have debt coming from many sources, it’s not advisable to plan your finances in this manner. Limit the total amount
of debt you have, and your credit-score will improve over time.
 Opt for longer repayment periods
Most people miss EMI payments because they are not in a stable enough position to repay their debts. The solution? Take longer tenures! They require smaller monthly payments, and allow you to be more consistent and flexible..
 Create a mix of credit types
Balance both secured and unsecured borrowings in your credit profile. Furthermore, show that you can capably handle both short, and long-term debt. This, in turn, shows credit agencies that you are a reliable borrower in a broader series of circumstances.
 Check your credit report
It is possible that credit rating agencies have made mistakes when it comes to your score in particular, as a result, it’s critical to go through these reports with a fine-toothed comb, in order to ensure that your score is an accurate reflection of your real-creditworthiness.
