 Section 80CCD
This discusses the tax deductions available to taxpayers regarding investments through the National Pension System.

There are two subsections here to be aware of:
Section 80CCD (1)
Investments in NPS are eligible for tax deductions under this section. Any Indian citizen between the age of 18 and 60 years can invest in NPS and avail this tax benefit, including NRI’s. The maximum benefit you can avail every year under this section is ₹1.5 lakh.

The maximum deduction you can avail under this section is 10% of your salary. For self-employed individuals, the limit is 20% of their gross total income.
Section 80CCD (1)
This subsection provides an additional deduction of ₹50,000 on investments in NPS. This is over and above the ₹1.5 lakh available under Section 80CCD(1). Allowing you to avail a total tax deduction of ₹2 lakh when you invest in NPS every year.
 Section 80D
Under this section, one can claim deductions up to ₹1 lakh for contributions towards medical insurance premiums bought for insuring self, spouse, children and parents. The deductions under 80D are over and above exemptions you can claim under Section 80C.
 Section 80E
Balance both secured and unsecured borrowings in your credit profile. Furthermore, show that you can capably handle both short, and long-term debt. This, in turn, shows credit agencies that you are a reliable borrower in a broader series of circumstances.
 Section 80EEA
Section 80EEA of the Income Tax Act. 1961 allows a deduction upto Rs. 1.5 lacs from income in respect of interest paid on loan taken for residential house by a first-time homebuyer where loan is sanctioned between 1st April, 2019 and 31st March, 2022 & the stamp duty value of house property does not exceed Rs. 45 lacs.
