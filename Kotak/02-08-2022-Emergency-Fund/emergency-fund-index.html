Securing your future early is a responsible approach to financial planning. The earlier you start planning, the more stability you’ll see later on. Don’t know where to start? We’re here to set you on the right track in your financial journey!
What is an emergency fund?
An emergency fund is an accumulation of funds, saved expressly
for the purpose of use in any unforeseen events. A general rule of
thumb is to have an emergency fund of at-least 6 months, to cover expenses, and EMI commitments saved-up at any time.
Where to start?
Savings Account:
Featuring much lower interest rates than other modes of saving, what sets a standard savings account apart is the ease at which your money
can be accessed.
Fixed Deposits
FD’s are a great method of investment which secure your funds, and provide attractive returns as well. Do note, that any returns on FD investments are taxable.
Liquid Funds
A low-risk and tax-efficient form of investment. The risk is extremely low with this form of investment. There's no lock-in period and funds can be redeemed within 1 working day.
Here’s how you can save smarter:
50:30:20 Rule A general rule of investment is to dedicate 50% of your funds to your needs, 30% to your wants, and invest or save 20%. While this may not be applicable in every individual scenario, it’s a solid rule of thumb upon which to base your investment strategy!
Develop a habit of budgeting: Before every paycheck, allocate your future resources, and in the month itself, try your best to not exceed limits.
Track expenses, and categories of them:
Once spending has begun, track where and how your money is moving, and if you find yourself in a spot of bother, analyze your spending to see where the problem lies.
Focus on quality purchases: A large volume of purchases can put holes in your plans to save. Instead, make fewer, bigger purchases which will serve to last you longer.
ONLY get a Credit Card if you can stay on-top of it:
Credit Cards can affect your credit score, making it advisable to only harness the use of one if you are in a financially stable state.
